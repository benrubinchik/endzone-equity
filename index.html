Our Client

Wharton Global HSIC 2025–26 Case Study

Connor Barwin, WG'23

Make the World Better Foundation

Connor Barwin is a former NFL linebacker who played for the Philadelphia Eagles. After retiring from football in 2019, he completed his MBA at the Wharton School in 2023. In 2013, Connor and his family established the Make the World Better (MTWB) foundation in Philadelphia, dedicated to revitalizing public spaces and strengthening communities by building inclusive, safe, and inspiring places for people to gather, play, and thrive.

Over the past decade, MTWB has invested more than $30 million in capital improvements to public spaces across Philadelphia — from multi-purpose parks and athletic fields to community centers hosting creative writing programs, basketball leagues, and music events.

The Investment Challenge

Connor would like to grow the initial MTWB investment of $500,000 into a fund of at least $1.5 million over 10 years, supporting capital projects that revitalize public spaces and inspire long-lasting community pride. Starting at the end of year three, he will make an annual drawdown of $10,000 to support grassroots community programs.

By 2036, the fund will be ready to invest in a truly transformative capital project — one designed to leave a lasting, visible legacy for Philadelphia's neighborhoods. Improvements may include modernized facilities, safer and brighter environments with LED lighting, more comfortable spaces with enhanced seating and accessible entryways, and community-ready features like AV systems and portable stages for events and performances.

Every dollar invested reflects MTWB's values — community-first, locally rooted, and sustainability-minded.

"What we really hope to do is inspire people to believe and understand that they can truly make a difference — big or small — in their lives and in the lives of others."

— Connor Barwin

Semifinal Q&A

Unique questions from the semifinal round judges

Q1
Your sector rotation model predicts Technology will return 7.93% over 6 months, yet you've already seen 60–67% gains in your semiconductor holdings. How did you backtest this model, and what prevents it from simply chasing momentum that's already occurred?
The model was trained on 17 years of data and validated on 8 years of out-of-sample data, achieving R² values of 0.68–0.74. 12-month price momentum is only one of six inputs — the remaining five are macroeconomic indicators (PMI, CPI, yield curve spread, unemployment, and the Fed Funds Rate) that predict which sectors will lead over the next six months. The model learns when momentum continues versus reverses based on macroeconomic context. A key test was 2022, when technology had strong trailing momentum but the model correctly predicted a downturn driven by elevated inflation and rising rates. To prevent overreaction, the team averages the model's predicted sector weights with current S&P 500 sector weights, ensuring diversification while capturing forward-looking signals. The model is rebalanced every six months to match its forecasting horizon.
Q2
Your portfolio strategy is aggressive, especially because many of your ETF picks overlap with the same speculative themes. Given a 10-year horizon, how do you intend to hedge against extremely high correlational risk?
Given the client's return requirements — tripling principal over 10 years while funding $10K annual withdrawals — an all-equity approach is necessary. Traditional hedging through bonds would increase the probability of missing the objective. The ETFs demonstrate minimal direct overlap with individual holdings: VEA, VSS, and ESGE provide international exposure; XLB and XLU cover Materials and Utilities (absent from stock positions); and AVUV holds profitable small-cap value vs. speculative growth. Total individual holding overlap is less than 1%. Correlation risk is managed through six-month rebalancing based on macroeconomic conditions, and Dynamic Asset Allocation beginning in year 8 reduces equity to 75–80% if returns are ahead of target, reallocating to Treasury bonds. Dividend income funds the client's cash flow needs.
Q3
Your small-cap allocation represents 25% of the portfolio with an average market cap of only $1.4B across 10 stocks. What's your exit strategy if multiple positions need to be liquidated simultaneously during a market downdraft?
Several factors mitigate liquidity risk: (1) the long-term investment horizon minimizes the need to react to short-term volatility, and since the capital isn't tied to living expenses, the client can absorb it; (2) the portfolio needs exposure to parts of the economy not represented by top companies — the risk of excluding this market segment outweighs the liquidity premium; (3) defined sell triggers (consecutive quarters of negative operating cash flow or revenue declines) allow gradual position reduction before urgent sell-offs; (4) the modest portfolio size reduces liquidity problems even during stress. Small-caps provide access to a more growth-oriented part of the economy essential for achieving the 12.3% CAGR target.
Q4 — Reflection
Reflect on one decision your team made that you would approach differently if given the opportunity again.
The team initially built the Scouting Report Index to systematically analyze whether companies' values aligned with the client's mission, but never verified whether those companies had a real impact on the community. In retrospect, the SRI should have been cross-referenced with ESG scores and validated through a secondary process. The competition taught the team to think less in terms of right or wrong and more in terms of probability — focusing on the range of outcomes, downside protection, scenario analysis, and position sizing. The primary goal of investing is not only to maximize returns but to construct a portfolio that can withstand unfavorable conditions while enabling long-term growth.
